Reception
  • Oxford University Museum of Natural History
  • 6 July 2019, 6:00PM - 12:00AM

It's party time with the dinosaurs! Meander here from the Oxford Town Hall, taking in some of Oxford's most beautiful sights, including the Radcliffe Camera, the Bodleian Library, the 'Bridge of Sighs', and some of the colleges too. If the weather's good, we'll have some drinks out on the lawn before heading in for dinner. If the wedding-related events get a bit...less than interesting, then feel free to wander amongst the exhibits and find your favourite fossils. And make sure you wear comfortable shoes for dancing (Sabrina could be tempted into leading an impromptu Zumba class if you're lucky!).

Directions

We’ve marked the useful transport options on the map below, plus a few places to eat, things to do and places to stay (also check University Rooms. Purple = wedding venues; Green= transport; Blue = parking; Yellow = cafes, museums, etc.
By bus/coach: If you’re coming from London, then Oxford Tube or X90 are the most straighforward options. They’re basically exactly the same in terms of price and stops, just run by different companies. Both leave London from Victoria Coach Station, picking up from Marble Arch, Baker Street (for X90 only) and Hillingdon, and stopping at lots of places in Oxford. St. Aldates is the most convenient stop for the Town Hall. Most buses will terminate at Gloucester Green in Oxford city centre.
By train: The train station is a little further from the Town Hall, about 10 minutes’ walk. Definitely book in advance to avoid exorbitant prices. There are some works going on across the lines through Oxford, so keep an eye on this before booking. You can also get a train from London Marylebone to Oxford Parkway, which is also cheaper in advance. You will need to get a taxi or a city bus across town though, so do your sums to make sure that it is actually cheaper!
By car: We strongly recommend not driving to Oxford, as it can be a bit of a pain to park. If you’re staying overnight, then park at your hotel. Otherwise, the Park & Ride is probably the cheapest option – there’s five of them around Oxford, and they’re marked on the map below with the blue parking symbol. If you want to park in the city (especially if you’re not staying overnight) then the Westgate Car Park is apparently the cheapest, and is marked in blue below as well.
